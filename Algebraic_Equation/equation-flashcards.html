Part 1 — Solving an equation

Step 1 Cancel the denominators
(x + 1) / 2 = x − 2 × 2 on both sides ↓ x + 1 = 2(x − 2)

Multiply both sides by the denominator.
More than one fraction? Use the LCM first.

Step 2 Click to reveal
Step 2 Expand the brackets
3(x − 10) = 2x − 10 expand ↓ 3x − 30 = 2x − 10

Multiply everything inside the bracket:
3 × x and 3 × (−10).

Step 3 Click to reveal
Step 3 x-terms on the left,
constants on the right
2x + 1 = 7 move it across (sign flips) ↓ 2x = 7 − 1

Cross the "=" and the sign flips:
+ ↔ −, × ↔ ÷.

Step 4 Click to reveal
Step 4 Combine like terms
x − 2x = −4 − 1 combine ↓ −x = −5 x = 5

Simplify each side to get ax = b, then divide by the coefficient of x.

Full example — all 4 steps together
(x + 1) / 2 = x − 2
STEP 1x + 1 = 2(x − 2)  (× 2 on both sides)
STEP 2x + 1 = 2x − 4  (expand the bracket)
STEP 3x − 2x = −4 − 1  (x-terms left, constants right)
STEP 4−x = −5  (combine) → x = 5

Part 2 — Word problems

Click each step to reveal what to do, with an example.

Step 1 Click to reveal
Step 1 Decide what to "Let x be"
Let Charles be x years old now

Pick the quantity everything else is compared to — usually the smallest / base one.
Remember the units (years old, cm, …).

Step 2 Click to reveal
Step 2 Express everything else in terms of x
"William is twice as old as Charles" so ↓ William is 2x years old now

Turn every other quantity into an expression with x — no new letters!

Step 3 Click to reveal
Step 3 Use the given information to form an equation
"10 years ago, William was 3 times as old as Charles" so ↓ 2x − 10 = 3(x − 10)

Find the sentence that says two things are equal — that sentence is your "=".

Step 4 Click to reveal
Step 4 Solve it with Part 1's steps
2x − 10 = 3(x − 10) Part 1: Steps 1 → 4 ↓ x = 20

Clear fractions → expand → move terms → combine.
Then answer the original question (with units!).

Full example — all 4 steps together
William is twice as old as Charles now. 10 years ago, William was 3 times as old as Charles. How old are they now?
STEP 1Let Charles be x years old now.
STEP 2Then William is 2x years old now. (10 years ago: Charles was x − 10, William was 2x − 10.)
STEP 32x − 10 = 3(x − 10)
STEP 4Solve with Part 1:
2x − 10 = 3x − 30  (expand the brackets)
2x − 3x = −30 + 10  (x-terms left, constants right)
−x = −20  (combine) → x = 20
∴ Charles is 20 years old and William is 40 years old now.
